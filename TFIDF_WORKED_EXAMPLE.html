TF = Term Frequency — how often a word appears in this course.  IDF = Inverse Document Frequency — how rare the word is across all courses. Multiply them: a word scores high only when it's frequent here and distinctive overall.

Drill-down · where the 0.664 course-weight comes from

Take just the word learning. Its weight inside a course = (TF × IDF) ÷ document length — count it, scale it up by how rare it is, then scale it down by the course's overall size so a long course doesn't win just for having more words.

THE HONEST CATCH

TF–IDF rewards overlap, not coverage

Notice the winner covers only 2 of Riya's 5 gap skills (machine learning + deep learning) — yet still ranks first, because it matches those words heavily. TF–IDF has no idea Riya needs all five, and it only matches exact words — a course that said "ML" instead of "machine learning" would score 0.

This is the whole reason the project continues: M5 (embeddings) fixes the exact-word problem — it understands that "ML" ≈ "machine learning". M6 (precision@k) then measures, with numbers, which recommender actually gives better results. TF–IDF is the honest baseline you compare against.
Say it in one breath: "Turn every course into a bag of weighted words, turn Riya's gap into the same kind of bag, then score each course by how much its distinctive words overlap with hers. Yoga shares nothing → 0. Deep Learning shares the most → top."
